# Employment transitions — the "why did you leave?" narratives

Interview-prep grounding for the job-change story. **Interview-prep ONLY — never
render any of this on a résumé or in a cover letter.** These are clean, honest,
externally-verifiable answers to the screen-stage "why did you leave / why so
short?" question.

> Note: the operator persona ("Jordan Avery") is a fictional engineer used to
> demonstrate the engine. The arc below is synthetic example data, not anyone's
> real history.

## The CAD-reseller → Lattice Additive → independent-practice arc

Two sub-18-month stints (a CAD/PLM reseller, then Lattice Additive) look like a flag
on paper. The story resolves it in ten seconds and is actually a positive:

- **CAD/PLM reseller:** crushing it — surpassed annual quota ahead of schedule.
  Under-challenged.
- **Reseller → Lattice Additive was a recruit, not a jump.** Lattice Additive and the
  reseller were **partner organizations**, and Lattice Additive **recruited the
  candidate away** for a bigger, more technical enterprise book. Framing: poached by a
  channel partner for performance, and took it because it was a real challenge and a
  learning opportunity. The opposite of instability.
- **Lattice Additive:** grew the large majority of strategic aerospace accounts,
  eight-figure new contracts.
- **The Lattice Additive departure was a mass layoff, not a choice.** Let go when the
  company cut a large share of staff — a company-wide RIF tied to additive-sector
  financial pressure. External, blameless, the kind of event that shows up in public
  reporting.

**Ten-second screen answer (rough shape):** "I was recruited into Lattice Additive
from a partner company for a bigger enterprise book — grew the large majority of our
aerospace accounts — until I was caught in a company-wide layoff. Since then I've run
an independent practice and built [the platform]."

Do NOT over-explain. The HM is checking "is this a flight risk / was there a
problem." A confident one-liner closes the loop; a long explanation creates concern.

## Why this works as a story (not a liability)

This narrative completely defuses the tenure flag, and it's a good story:

- **"Recruited away by a partner org for a bigger enterprise book" is a performance
  signal, the opposite of instability.** Companies poach people who are winning.
- **The Lattice Additive exit wasn't the candidate leaving — it was a company-wide
  RIF.** External, blameless, the cleanest possible "why did you leave."

So the two sub-18-month stints read as: **poached up through partner organizations,
then caught in a mass layoff.** An HM hears that in ten seconds and moves on — it's
resolved, even sympathetic. The thing that *looked* like the weakness is actually
fine. A layoff in a sector-wide downturn is not a verdict on ability: the candidate
was wanted *because* they were good, grew the hardest accounts, and then a company
under financial pressure cut a large share of everyone.
