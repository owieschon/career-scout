# The 2024-2026 Hiring Landscape

*The market has bifurcated. AI-native companies are hiring hard for specific roles at inflated comp. Traditional B2B SaaS is in a "do more with less" posture with harder quotas and less generous comp packages. Remote roles still exist but have shrunk meaningfully since the 2021-2022 peak.*

## The Post-ZIRP Correction

From roughly 2020 through early 2022, the zero-interest-rate-policy era drove a hiring boom that priced senior IC roles at multiples of pre-pandemic comp. Companies hired against pipeline they didn't have yet. Engineering bands hit absurd levels (Senior eng at $300K+ TC at mid-size SaaS). Sales hiring was aggressive on headcount with looser quota expectations.

The correction started in mid-2022 with the first major layoff waves (Meta, Stripe, Shopify) and continued through 2023-2024 with broad-based reductions across tech. By mid-2024 the market had reset:

- **Comp compression** at the high end. Senior IC TC at mid-size SaaS came down from peak 2021 levels by roughly 15-25%. Base salaries held up better than equity.
- **Bar-raising in interviews.** Companies that hired loosely in 2021 are now more selective, often running longer interview loops and higher-stakes take-homes.
- **Headcount discipline.** "Do more with less" became the explicit management posture. Open reqs are scrutinized harder. Backfills are not automatic.
- **Layoff overhang.** The pool of recently laid-off candidates is large and includes very strong people, which pushes the median bar up at any given comp level.

The 2026 picture is more nuanced than the 2024 panic. Hiring has selectively recovered, but it's concentrated in specific areas (see below). The "do more with less" posture is still dominant outside of AI-native companies.

## The AI Hiring Surge

The single largest counterweight to the broader correction is AI-driven hiring. From roughly late 2022 (ChatGPT launch) through 2026, AI-native companies and AI-product teams at incumbents have driven a distinct hiring cycle:

- **OpenAI, Anthropic, Mistral, Cohere, xAI, Inflection (folded into Microsoft), various stealth labs**: aggressive hiring with comp packages at or above peak 2021 levels for senior roles. ML researchers and applied AI engineers at the top of the band command $400K-$1M+ TC at the frontier labs.
- **AI-native application companies** (Quill Code, Perplexity, Lexicon AI, Lumen Search, Hebbia, etc.): hiring across engineering, GTM, and product at premium comp.
- **Incumbent AI teams** (Salesforce Einstein, Snowflake Cortex, Atlassian Intelligence, etc.): selectively hiring AI specialists at comp premiums to their general bands.
- **Forward Deployed Engineer roles**: emerged as the highest-leverage GTM-adjacent role at AI companies. Comp packages can rival senior PM/EM bands.

The pull on senior IC talent into these companies has been significant. The trickle-down effect: mid-market B2B SaaS companies trying to compete for AI-fluent talent have had to bend their bands, often unsuccessfully.

## Remote Work in 2026

The 2021 peak of "fully remote forever" companies has unwound substantially. The 2024-2025 push to return-to-office was real and broad:

- **Apple, Google, Meta, Amazon** all tightened to 3-5 days/week in office by 2024. Amazon went to 5 days in early 2025.
- **Salesforce, Dell, IBM, others** followed with various 3-day mandates.
- **The "remote tax"** emerged at some companies: lower comp for fully-remote roles vs. hub-located roles. Some companies framed this as "geo-adjustment"; some made it explicit policy.

What's still fully remote in 2026 (best estimate, may be partially out of date):
- **Smaller, founder-led startups** under ~50 people, especially those founded post-2020
- **Specific remote-first companies**: GitLab, Automattic, Doist, Buffer, 37signals, Zapier (mostly), Toptal, Hopper
- **Most of the AI-native wave** above, though increasingly with "hub" preferences in SF/NYC
- **Specific functions at hybrid companies**: customer success, certain sales roles, some engineering teams have stayed remote even at companies that returned to office

What's largely gone remote-only:
- Most large enterprise tech companies
- Most companies that scaled past 500 people post-2021

For a remote-only candidate, the practical implication: the available pool of roles has shrunk meaningfully from the 2021 peak. The remaining pool is concentrated in smaller companies, specific remote-first organizations, and certain function/seniority combinations. Patience and selectivity matter more.

## Geographic Comp Adjustments

The "geo-adjusted comp" trend that emerged 2020-2021 has stuck partially:

- Some companies (Google, Meta) implemented and largely maintained tiered comp by location
- Others (Netflix, some smaller companies) pay the same regardless of location
- For Columbus, OH-based candidates, the relevant data point: most companies treat Ohio as a "Tier 2" or "Tier 3" location, meaning 5-15% below SF/NYC bands. Some treat all US locations equally.

In practical terms for a candidate in Columbus, OH targeting an example band (e.g. $150k–$190k):
- Top-of-band roles are achievable but the specific company matters
- The same role at the same company can pay 10-20% less in Ohio than SF
- Negotiation leverage on the geo-adjustment exists but is limited

## Sales Hiring in 2026

The sales hiring landscape has bifurcated:

- **AE roles at mid-market B2B SaaS**: quotas got harder (typically up 15-25% from 2021-22 levels), bases held but accelerators tightened. The "easy money" era of 2021 closed. Mid-market AEs are quitting more, hitting quota less, and finding the next role harder.
- **Enterprise AE roles** at well-known companies: still relatively healthy. Lower volume of open roles, higher selectivity.
- **SDR/BDR roles consolidation**: the SDR role has been hit hard. Many companies have:
  - Cut SDR teams entirely
  - Replaced SDRs with AI tools (Clay, Apollo with AI sequences, Outreach automations)
  - Promoted SDRs into AE roles rather than backfill at the SDR level
  - Pushed AEs to do more of their own prospecting
- **Founding AE roles** at seed/Series A startups: high-leverage opportunity, often at lower base ($120K-$180K) with meaningful equity. Requires comfort with ambiguity and prospecting.

What's specific about sales hiring in 2026:
- Companies want demonstrated quota attainment in this market, not 2021 numbers. A candidate who hit 110% in 2024 is more valuable than one who hit 130% in 2021.
- The "I sold to manufacturing/industrial" specialty is back in demand as companies expand into less-saturated verticals.
- Cold outbound experience matters more than warm-inbound velocity, because most companies' inbound has slowed.

## RevOps as a Growth Area

RevOps has been one of the few sustained hiring tailwinds through the correction:

- **Operational rigor over enablement.** The trend is toward RevOps teams that ship systems (forecasting models, attribution, lead routing, comp models) rather than RevOps teams that build training content.
- **Technical depth required.** Modern RevOps roles increasingly expect SQL, dbt, sometimes Python. The Salesforce admin path is being squeezed at the upper end; the analytics engineer / data engineer path is opening up.
- **Salesforce admins moving up the stack.** A common 2026 career path: Salesforce admin -> sales ops analyst -> revops manager. The technical adjacency to data engineering is real.
- **AI-augmented RevOps work**: pipeline scoring, deal risk prediction, automated reporting. The teams that have built this in-house are the ones hiring.
- **Comp bands**: Senior RevOps IC at $130K-$200K base. RevOps Manager at $150K-$220K base. RevOps Director / Head of $200K-$300K base. Total comp depends heavily on equity at smaller companies.

This is one of the rare functions where "more technical" maps cleanly to "higher comp" at the IC level.

## Forward Deployed / Solutions / Applied AI

These roles emerged from Palantir-style customer engineering and exploded at AI-native companies. In 2026, this is the highest-leverage hiring category:

- **Palantir Forward Deployed Engineer**: the original. Embeds with customers, ships custom integrations and analyses. Comp at senior level $200K-$400K TC.
- **OpenAI / Anthropic Forward Deployed**: similar model, applied to LLM deployments at enterprise customers. Comp $250K-$500K+ TC.
- **AI startups (Lumen Search, Lexicon AI, Hebbia, Quill Code, etc.)**: variously labeled "Forward Deployed Engineer," "Solutions Engineer," "Applied AI Engineer," "Customer Engineer." Function is roughly: own the technical relationship with key accounts, ship custom work as needed, drive adoption.
- **Comp can rival senior PM/EM bands.** At top AI-native companies, $200K-$300K base with equity that has real upside.

The role requirements typically include:
- Ability to write production code (not just prototypes)
- Comfort with customer-facing work
- Domain knowledge in the customer's industry helpful but not required at the most generalist roles
- Specifically at AI-native companies: deep familiarity with LLMs, prompt engineering, eval design

The interview loops for these roles tend to be intense (often 5-7 rounds including a take-home build) but the bar is more about "can you ship and talk to humans" than "can you do leetcode."

## What's Slow

Areas where hiring is meaningfully slower than 2021:

- **Traditional enterprise CS** (large-account success managers at established SaaS companies). The function got over-hired in 2020-2022 and has been steadily trimmed.
- **Low-touch SDR/BDR**: see above. Being replaced by tooling and consolidated into AE roles.
- **Mid-level marketing**: especially content marketing, demand gen at the manager level. AI-driven content production has compressed entry-level needs.
- **Project/program management without a technical specialty**: still hired but bar is higher, comp is flatter.
- **Junior product managers** without a technical or design background. Senior PMs still in demand.

For the operator's relevant tracks, the slow areas to be aware of:
- Track 3 (TAM/CS): the field is real but the volume of open roles is lower than 2022. Specialization (technical CS at AI companies, CS at hard-tech companies) is the differentiator.
- Track 1 (AE): real demand but quotas are harder. Selectivity on the company's pipeline health matters more than ever.

## Contract and Fractional Work in 2026

The contract/fractional landscape:

- **More common than in 2022.** The acceptance of fractional executives (fractional CFO, fractional Head of RevOps, fractional CMO) has gone mainstream at sub-100-person companies.
- **Rate compression vs 2021 peak.** Hourly rates for senior contractors peaked in 2021-2022 and came down by roughly 10-20% by 2024. Recovery has been uneven.
- **Typical rates 2026 (estimate)**:
  - Senior IC contract: $100-$200/hour
  - Fractional manager/director: $150-$300/hour
  - Fractional executive: $200-$500/hour
- **Engagement patterns**: 10-20 hours/week is the most common fractional model. Project-based engagements (3-6 month delivery scopes) are also common.
- **Health insurance and benefits**: still the structural disadvantage of contract work. ACA marketplace is the standard fallback.

For a runway-extending bridge engagement, the math typically works at $100K-$150K annualized for 15-20 hours/week, leaving time for the full-time job search.

## What Hiring Managers Worry About Now

The specific concerns HMs voice in 2026 that weren't dominant in 2021:

1. **"I over-hired in 2021 and got burned."** HMs who were burned by aggressive hiring in the ZIRP era are now extra-cautious about adding headcount they can't justify.
2. **"AI hype-train resumes."** Candidates whose resumes claim deep AI/ML work that they can't back up in technical conversation. The volume of these candidates has risen sharply since 2023.
3. **"Can they ship without an LLM."** A real concern at engineering hiring: candidates who have only ever coded with Copilot/Claude and can't reason about systems independently. Coming up in interview loop design.
4. **"Will they actually move the number."** A return to first-principles evaluation: forget the resume, can this person produce a result. Trial projects and reference checks are heavier in 2026.
5. **"Are they going to leave in 9 months for a higher offer at an AI company."** Retention risk for senior IC and management talent who might be poached by frontier labs.
6. **"Is this candidate's prior comp inflated by 2021 bubble pricing."** HMs are skeptical of "I made $300K at my last role" claims and often pressure-test the base/equity split.

## What This Means for the Operator

- **The Track 4 (Forward Deployed / Applied AI) bet is the right one.** This is where comp is highest, hiring is most active, and an "I ship production code with AI coding tools" story directly addresses the "can they actually build" concern HMs are voicing.
- **Track 2 (RevOps) is the second-strongest bet structurally.** Specifically RevOps with technical depth (SQL, Python, AI augmentation). The Salesforce-admin-only RevOps market is being squeezed, but the Python/ML work pushes the candidate toward the high end of the band.
- **For Track 1 (AE), the manufacturing/industrial domain specialty is a real lever.** Generic B2B SaaS AE is crowded and quotas are hard. AE roles at companies selling to manufacturing buyers (where the Lattice Additive / desktop 3D-printing credentials translate) are less crowded.
- **The remote-first preference is a real filter in 2026.** Half of attractive companies will be hybrid-or-RTO. Don't waste cycles on those. Build a working list of companies that are genuinely remote-first.
- **Remote-first roles are travel-light by default.** Most fully-remote SaaS roles don't require travel. The disclosure script handles the edge cases.
- **Contract/fractional bridge work is realistic at $100-$150K annualized for 15-20 hours/week.** This is a viable runway-extending move while the full-time search continues, and the Cadence Analytics work is a credible portfolio piece for RevOps consulting engagements.
